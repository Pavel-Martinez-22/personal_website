Green Chile Mac N Cheese

I love to cook but I am not very creative, so I rely on cookbooks to help me create amazing meals at home. I am Hispanic so I like a little zest in my food, in my search for more Hispanic recipes I have come across the best tasting Mac and Cheese I have ever had and I would like to share this recipe with you. This recipe comes from the book, “The Essential Mexican Instant Pot Cookbook.” I highly suggest this book if you enjoy this recipe. Here is a link encase you might want to purchase the book after trying this recipe, “The Essential Mexican Instant Pot Cookbook.”

Recipe Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 small white onion, diced
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced (2 tablespoons)
  • 1 jalapeno chile, stemmed and minced (with seeds)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or chicken broth
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 5 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 tablespoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 pound large elbow macaroni
  • ½ cup Mexican crema or sour cream
  • 2 cups of shredded mild cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded Monterey Jack or Pepper Jack cheese
  • Poblano chiles, roasted, seeded, peeled, and cut into 1-inch dice
  • Thinly sliced green onions, chopped for serving
  • Fresh Cilantro leaves for serving

Recipe Instructions

This recipe will require the use of a pressure cooker.

  1. You will begin by pressing the sauté button on the pressure cooker and melt the butter. Add the onion, garlic, and jalapeno and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 3 minutes, until softened but not browned. Then Press the cancel button and add the broth, cream, salt pepper, and macaroni, then stir to combine.
  2. Secure the lid and set the pressure release to sealing. Press pressure cook button, and then set the cooking time for eight minutes.
  3. When the cooking program is complete, press cancel. Perform a quick pressure release by moving the pressure release to venting. Open the pot, then add the crema, cheddar cheese, and Pepper Jack cheese and stir until the cheeses has melted.
  4. Stir the poblano chiles then replace the lid without securing it. Let stand for two minutes. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.
  5. Serve the mac and cheese right away, sprinkled with the green onions and cilantro leaves.

Schneider, D. M. (2018). The essential Mexican Instant Pot cookbook: Authentic flavors and modern recipes for your electric pressure cooker. California: Ten Speed Press.
